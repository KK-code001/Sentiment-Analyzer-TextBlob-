Technology in education has both positive and negative effects, making it an interesting topic for analysis. On the positive side, technology has made learning easier, faster, and more accessible. Students can attend online classes, watch recorded lectures, and use educational apps to improve their understanding. Smart boards, videos, and virtual labs make lessons more engaging and less boring than traditional methods. Teachers also benefit because they can prepare lessons quickly, conduct online tests, and manage student records efficiently. These improvements save time and increase productivity.

However, technology also creates several problems. Many students become distracted by social media, games, and unnecessary internet usage instead of focusing on studies. Excessive screen time can cause health issues such as eye strain and lack of concentration. In some areas, students still do not have proper internet access or digital devices, which creates inequality in education. Overdependence on technology may also reduce critical thinking and personal interaction between teachers and students.

Overall, technology has improved education significantly, but it must be used wisely. It is helpful when it supports learning, but harmful when it becomes a source of distraction. The real impact depends on how students and teachers choose to use it.
